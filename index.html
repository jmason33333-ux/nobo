02 · How it works

A system, configured around your business.

Five layers. Each one carries the context the next one needs. Together they make Claude sound like someone who's been here a while.

01

Context that makes it yours

I write down who your business is. Your clients, your promises, how you sound on a good day. The system reads it before it answers anything, so Claude stops sounding like a stranger and starts sounding like someone who's been here a while.

→ your voice · your clients · your promises
→ the docs, threads, and quirks that define the work
02

Specialists who own the work

Each role on your team gets a named specialist that knows what they own. Account Lead. Chief of Staff. Marketing. Ops. Each one carries the context for that piece of the business and stays in their lane.

→ named roles, not generic chat
→ each one carries context for what they own
03

Skills that do the work

Real jobs your business needs done, drafted to your standard, delivered with receipts. Some run on a cue, some run on a schedule. All of them leave the work in better shape than they found it.

→ prep a call · draft a proposal · close the day
→ ships a result, not a reply
04

Connectors to the tools you already use

Gmail. Drive. Calendar. Slack. Notion. The system reaches into the surfaces your team already lives in. Nothing new to install, nothing new to learn, your data stays where it is.

→ Gmail · Drive · Calendar · Slack · Notion
→ every connector Cowork adds, your system grows with
05

Outcomes that compound

Every Friday the system reads its own logs and proposes its own upgrades. You approve. It compounds. By month three, the system knows more about how your team works than your last hire did.

this week → caught a pattern you'd been living with for months
next week → sharper than last week always
