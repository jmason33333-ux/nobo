Honest answers.

Why does it feel like we're still stuck at square one?

Most teams plateau. More prompts, more back-and-forth, grinding through the same loop without ever landing on what good actually looks like for your business. That's not a Claude problem. That's a system problem. What I build defines the standard once — your voice, your context, your expectations — so every output starts from the right place.

Why does every session feel like Claude has never met us before?

Because it hasn't. Claude doesn't retain anything between sessions by default. It doesn't know your clients, your process, or how you like things done. Once we build that layer in, your team picks up where they left off instead of starting over every time.

Why does it take more work to explain what I need than to just do it myself?

You're doing the work twice. That's the sign of a system that isn't really a system yet. The goal isn't just getting Claude to follow instructions — it's getting you to the point where you trust it enough to actually delegate. That's what we build toward.

We've tried to automate things before. Why does it always end up breaking?

Usually because it was held together with too many pieces and no one really owned it. What I build has fewer moving parts, solid documentation, and I stay involved after handoff. This isn't a build-it-and-disappear situation.

Is this actually worth it for a team our size?

Small teams feel the cost of manual work more than anyone. Every hour spent on something that could run itself is an hour you're not spending on clients, strategy, or growth. If I don't think it'll move the needle for you, I'll tell you in the first conversation.
